Fire warms you, cooks food, and boils water clean. It is never free, and keeping one alive costs less than starting a new one.

## Making fire from friction

There is no flint-and-steel. The first fire comes from friction:

- **Crouch and hold right-click** with a stick on the ground to drill for an ember.
- Drilling alone only makes **smoke** — it won't catch.
- Lighting a fire requires **tinder** on hand: a bundle of **plant fiber**, or dry **leaf litter**. The tinder is **consumed** when the fire catches.
- **Wet materials and rain make it fail.** Drilling needs dry materials and dry ground.

## The campfire

The campfire is the hearth. It is **crafted from sticks over logs** — no coal needed.

Once lit, it runs on fuel and needs feeding:

- **Right-click it with sticks, logs, or planks** to add fuel and make it burn longer.
- It **burns down over time and goes out** when the fuel runs dry.
- It gives off **warmth that dwindles as the fuel runs low** — a dying fire warms less.
- It **cooks food** and **boils water** (set a fire-safe vessel over it — see [Water](./water-and-drinking.md)).

## Carrying an ember

Fire can be carried between camps instead of drilling a fresh one:

- **Sneak and right-click a lit campfire empty-handed** to scoop out a glowing **ember**. This costs the fire a little fuel.
- The ember **cools as it is carried**. Its durability-style bar shows the remaining glow — it lasts about **3.3 minutes** before dying to cold charcoal.
- **Right-clicking the ground** with a live ember starts a **fresh, already-lit campfire** — no drilling, no tinder needed.
- If the ember runs out before it is used, it dies.
