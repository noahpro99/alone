With a strict 1 m³ limit on your person, moving more than a single block at a time means gear — and the backpack is your first real answer.

## The backpack

**Craft a backpack** from **leather + string**. It's a full storage container you carry with you.

- **Right-click to open** a **27-slot pack**. Its contents ride on the item itself and show in the **tooltip**, so you can see what's inside at a glance.
- A **quick-open keybind** (default **B**) opens the **first pack in your inventory** without digging for it.
- **Set it down as a block.** Sneak + right-click a surface to place the pack, then right-click it to open like a chest. **Break it to get the pack back with all its contents** intact.

### Backpacks and your carry cap

A backpack **raises your carry volume cap by about 1.5 m³** — but there's an honest catch:

- **Its contents count toward that cap.** The pack gives you more room, but filling it uses that room up. It lets you carry **more** without letting you **overstuff** — you can't cheat the volume system by pocketing a pack full of blocks.

See [Weight, Carrying & Blocks](./building-and-blocks.md) for how the volume cap works.

## Armor doesn't cost you volume

**Worn armor doesn't count against your volume cap** — it rides on your body, not in your pack. You can wear a full set *and* still pick up a block.

Its **weight still slows you**, though, so a full suit of plate is a real load to move around in even if it doesn't eat your carrying space.
