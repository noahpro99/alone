Eating keeps your hunger up, but *what*, *how*, and *when* you eat all matter — raw food can sicken you, meals take real time, and a monotonous diet stops filling you up.

## Raw food is risky

Eating raw meat can give you a foodborne illness. Cooking makes food **safe**, so cook before you eat whenever you can.

| Raw food | Sickness chance |
| --- | --- |
| Chicken (and other poultry/pork) | ~80% |
| Red meat (beef, mutton) | ~45% |
| Fish | ~20% |
| Rotten flesh | Near-guaranteed |

Getting sick means a lingering illness that leaves you weak and hungry — a real condition, not a brief potion tick. A cooked meal carries none of this risk.

## Eating takes time and discipline

You can't wolf food down mid-scramble:

- **You can't eat while sprinting.** Stop first.
- Eating takes **real time** — about **3× longer than vanilla**, so a snack takes roughly **5 seconds**. You have to commit to the meal, which means finding a safe moment.

## Spoilage

Perishable foods **rot to rotten flesh after about one in-game day**. Fresh meat and produce won't keep — plan to eat, cook, or preserve them before they turn.

## Preserving food with salt

Salt lets you keep perishable food through the winter:

- Hold a perishable food, keep **salt in your off hand**, and **sneak + right-click** to salt it.
- The food is then preserved and **won't spoil** — it keeps indefinitely.
- Each salting **costs 1 salt**. Salt comes from **boiling salt water** (see [Water](./water-and-drinking.md)).

This ties the coast to your winter stores: boil seawater for salt, salt your surplus, and eat through the cold months.

## Nutrition variety and food fatigue

Foods belong to groups — **protein, vegetable, grain, fruit**. Eat the same group over and over and you burn out on it:

- Repeatedly eating one group **raises that group's fatigue**.
- High fatigue **shrinks your usable maximum hunger** (down to roughly 12), so you simply can't fill up as much on a monotonous diet.
- **Eating a variety** lets the fatigue recover and restores your full hunger cap.

Keep a mixed diet — meat, veg, grain, and fruit — to stay well fed.

## Food and your other meters

What you eat also nudges warmth and thirst:

- **Hot meals warm you.** Eating a cooked meal or stew pushes your body temperature toward comfort (it can't overheat you), then drifts back over about 40 seconds — a handy tool against winter cold alongside [fire](./fire.md) and shelter.
- **Food affects thirst:**
  - **Salt-preserved** food **dehydrates** you (a noticeable water cost) — the trade-off for winter stores.
  - **Dry** foods (bread, cookies, dried kelp, rotten flesh) cost a little water.
  - **Juicy** foods (fruit, melon, soups) **give water back**.

Stockpiling salted food means stockpiling water too.

## Hygiene — wash your hands

Handling raw meat makes a mess:

- **Butchering dirties your hands.**
- **Eating with dirty hands adds ~25% sickness** to any food, even cooked.
- **Wash by drinking water** — a drink cleans your hands.

So wash up before you eat after a butchering session, or you'll taint an otherwise safe meal.

## Hunger and stamina

Hunger gates your recovery: **below 6 hunger, your stamina recovers poorly.** Running yourself hungry doesn't just risk starvation — it leaves you unable to catch your breath. Keep fed to keep moving.
