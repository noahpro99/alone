The world runs on a turning year, keeps its monsters penned near loot, and won't hand you free light — so plan around the seasons, the map, and your torches.

## Seasons

The year cycles **spring → summer → winter**, with each season lasting about **7 in-game days**.

- **Winter** shifts the ambient temperature **colder**.
- **Summer** shifts it **hotter**.

Dress and shelter for whatever season you're in — see [Body Temperature](./body-temperature.md) for beating winter cold and summer heat.

## Farming with the seasons

Your crops live and die by the calendar, so a field needs tending, not just planting:

- **Crops stop growing in winter.** Nothing advances until spring — though **bonemeal still works** if you want to force growth.
- **Crops can die on their own.** Each has a small per-tick chance to wither, and that chance is **higher in winter** and **higher next to weeds**.
- **Fields sow weeds.** Over time, crops scatter **weeds (short grass)** onto nearby soil. Left alone, the weeds crowd in and raise the death chance — so keep your rows clear.

The upshot: plant in spring and summer, weed your fields, and don't count on winter growth. More on food is on [Food & Eating](./food-and-eating.md).

## Monsters only near loot structures

The signature rule of the wilderness: **natural hostile spawns are blocked unless you're within about 40 blocks of a generated structure** — ruins, dungeons, mineshafts, strongholds, villages, and the like.

- The **open wilderness is lonely and relatively safe.** You can travel and camp in the wild without being swarmed at night.
- **Danger clusters at loot.** Anywhere worth looting is anywhere worth fearing.
- This only touches **natural** spawns. **Spawners, animal breeding, and passive wildlife are unaffected** — a dungeon spawner still works, farms still breed, and animals roam normally.

Plan expeditions to structures as the dangerous part of the game, and treat the wilds between them as safe ground.

## Torches burn out

Torches are a **fuel item**, not an infinite light:

- **Craft** a torch from **coal + stick**, then **light it** at a fire source or with **flint & steel**.
- A **lit torch — placed or held — burns down over time** to a spent stub. Its remaining fuel is shown like a durability bar.
- **Mining a placed torch returns its remaining fuel** (as a torch with that much life left) — no free top-up, but no waste either.

Note: the **classic vanilla torch recipe is gone.** The burn-down torch is the only torch, so light management is now part of exploring — carry spares, and don't leave torches burning where you don't need them.

## The ground is a safe stash

**Dropped items never despawn.** Anything you drop or leave on the ground stays there indefinitely, so the world floor is a reliable stash — set things down without fear of losing them to the vanilla five-minute timer.
