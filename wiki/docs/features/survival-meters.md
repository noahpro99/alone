Your body is tracked by a small stack of bars and markers in the top-left corner of the screen — it stays quiet until something is wrong, then it tells you exactly what.

## Reading the HUD

The corner readout stacks a few bars, each with its own icon, plus a temperature square and condition markers off to the side. From top to bottom the bars are:

- **Vitality** (heart) — blood-red fading to amber. This replaces the vanilla hearts bar. See [Injuries, Conditions & Health](./injuries-and-conditions.md).
- **Stamina** (feather) — green. Your short-term wind for hard effort.
- **Endurance reserve** (feather) — how rested you are. Full means fresh; it drains as you tire over the day.
- **Hunger** (golden carrot) — how fed you are.
- **Thirst** (water bucket) — blue. Covered on [Water & Drinking](./water-and-drinking.md); summarized below.
- **Carried volume** (bundle) — grey to amber to red. How full your pack is.

To the right sits the **temperature square** with a warming/cooling arrow, and just below it the **condition markers** (see below).

## Stamina (the green bar)

Stamina is your immediate wind — the effort you can spend before you have to stop and breathe.

- It **drains** when you sprint, jump, swim, mine, chop wood, fight, and climb.
- A hard sprint empties the bar in about **40 seconds** — this is a real run, not a quick dash.
- It **recovers** when you rest and stop exerting, refilling over roughly **25 seconds** of standing still.
- When it runs **near-empty**, your body gives out: you're **slowed**, hit with **mining fatigue**, and **can't sustain a sprint** until you've caught your breath.

Practical tips:

- Don't sprint everywhere. Save your wind for when you need to cross open ground or flee.
- Mining and chopping cost stamina per swing, so a long quarrying session will wear you down — pace it.
- If you run out of stamina while climbing, **you fall** — never start a climb on an empty bar.

### Hunger gates recovery

Stamina only rebuilds properly when you're fed. **Below 6 hunger, stamina recovers poorly** — an empty stomach leaves you unable to catch your breath, so keep eating if you plan to work.

## Endurance reserve (fatigue)

Below the stamina bar is your **endurance reserve** — a slower, deeper gauge. Full means well-rested; it drains steadily as you tire across the day and through hard exertion. You shed fatigue and refill this reserve by **resting and sleeping** — see [Sleep & Rest](./sleep-and-rest.md).

## Thirst (the blue bar)

Thirst drains over time, faster when you're sprinting or your body runs hot. Drink to refill it, but **untreated water can make you sick** (roughly a **15% chance** from a raw fill, higher from a dirty vessel). Boiling water over a campfire makes it safe. Full details — vessels, water quality, salt water, and boiling — are on [Water & Drinking](./water-and-drinking.md).

## Carried volume (the grey/amber/red bar)

The bottom bar shows how full you are. Every item has a real **volume**, and you can carry a hard cap of about **one cubic metre** on your person — one full block, *or* a lot of small things.

- **Grey** — plenty of room.
- **Amber** — getting full.
- **Red** — at the cap; extra items simply won't be picked up (they stay in the world, nothing is dropped).

Weight is separate and softer: the heavier your load, the **slower you move**. One heavy block makes you trudge. Worn armor rides on your body, so it doesn't eat into your volume cap (though its weight still slows you). The full weight-and-volume system — chest limits, transport, building loads — is covered on the Building page.

## Realistic reach and honest work

- **Reach** is shortened to about **2.75 blocks** (down from vanilla's ~4.5). You have to be close to what you break or place. Creative keeps the long vanilla reach for building.
- **Break times are honest.** Mining stone with an iron pick takes around **20 seconds** (ore and deepslate longer, capped near 60s), and digging dirt, sand, or gravel takes a few seconds even with a shovel. Every block you break also **costs stamina** — quarrying is genuine labour, not a formality.

## Condition markers

Below the temperature square, small colored markers flag active conditions so you can treat them:

- **Sick** — green
- **Bleeding** — red
- **Sprained** — orange

These, plus infection and the paper-doll injury figure, are explained in full on [Injuries, Conditions & Health](./injuries-and-conditions.md).
