Crafting is real work here: you can't punch trees, crafting takes time and roots you in place, and metal gear must be forged and tempered by hand. This page covers how to get from bare hands to a full toolkit.

## No punching trees

Bare hands don't fell timber:

- **Bare hands, a chicken, or a block of dirt** do nothing to a log.
- A **sword or pickaxe** chops a log **very slowly** (~30 seconds) — a bad idea.
- An **axe** is the right tool: slow but functional (~10–15 seconds).

So your first job is an axe. That means flint.

## Day-one flint tools

Before any metal, you knap flint into primitive tools. Craft them from **flint + a stick**:

- **Flint hatchet** — fells trees. Your priority first craft.
- **Flint pick** — mines stone.
- **Flint knife** — a crude cutting blade (also strips plant fiber, below).

### Day-one tools quick list

1. **Flint hatchet** (flint + stick) — for felling trees.
2. **Flint pick** (flint + stick) — for mining stone.
3. **Flint knife** (flint + stick) — for cutting and stripping fiber.

Gather flint from gravel, grab some sticks, and you hold day-one human technology within minutes.

## Plant fiber and string

You don't need spiders for string:

- **Strip plant fiber** by tearing up grass, ferns, vines, and dead bushes. Bare hands get about a 50% chance for one strand; a **cutting blade** (sword, axe, hoe, or flint knife) strips **1–2 clean lengths** at once.
- **Twist 3 fiber into 1 string.**

String feeds the whole economy — rope, bandages, bows, and more.

## Timed, stationary crafting

A craft is no longer instant. Once a valid recipe sits in the grid, you must **work at it** before you can take the result:

- A **green progress bar** fills along the result slot (with a percentage on the action bar). You can't pick the result up until it's done.
- Times scale with the effort involved:

| Craft type | Time |
| --- | --- |
| Simple items (planks, torch) | ~2s |
| Food | ~5s |
| Tools & weapons | ~15s |
| Stations (chest, furnace) | ~60s |
| Armor | ~2 min |

- Crafting is **resumable** — progress is kept per item and survives closing the screen or switching recipes and back.
- **Taking a result also costs stamina.** Crafting is exertion, done in safety, not something you panic-craft mid-fight.

## Forge & temper — metal gear

Metal tools and armor aren't finished in the crafting grid. A grid-crafted iron or steel piece comes out a **brittle unforged blank** (barely usable) until you work it hot:

1. **Heat it** — hold the blank **by a lit forge** (blast furnace, furnace, smoker, campfire, or lava) and it glows hotter. Step away and it cools. You can only work it while hot.
2. **Hammer it** — with a **smithing hammer** in your pack, **right-click an anvil** to land a blow. A piece takes **many blows across several heats** — a dozen for a tool, up to twenty for a chestplate. Forging one piece is real, tended work.
3. **Quality** — when it finishes it rolls a **random quality**, from **crude → masterwork**, which sets its durability.
4. **Re-temper** — reheat and rehammer a finished piece to **reroll its quality**. But each rework **lowers the ceiling** on how good it can get — and also **fully repairs** the piece. Reworking is a gamble: chase a better edge at the cost of a blade that won't last as long.

The forge state and quality grade show in the item's tooltip; heat and blows show on the action bar as you work.

## Whetstone — maintaining an edge

Keep your tools sharp with a whetstone:

- **Craft a whetstone** from smooth stone + a stick.
- Hold a worn tool or weapon, keep the **whetstone in your off hand**, and **sneak + right-click** to re-hone the edge.
- Each pass **restores ~25% durability**, wears the whetstone a little, and has a short cooldown.
- Armor has no edge and can't be honed — only a forge **re-temper** repairs armor.

Sharpening an edge is cheap maintenance; only a forge re-temper changes a blade's underlying quality.

## Steel — the top tier

Steel replaces diamond as the best gear:

- **Blast an iron ingot** (in a blast furnace) into a **steel ingot**.
- **Steel tools** mine at diamond level with high durability, and **steel armor** sits above iron.
- Steel is the new top tier — the goal of the whole tool progression.

## Diamond gear is abolished

You can **no longer craft diamond or netherite tools and armor** — diamond would shatter as gear, so it's simply not craftable anymore. **Diamonds remain as items** (for blocks, trade, and other uses); they're just no longer the endgame material. **Steel is the intended top tier instead.**
