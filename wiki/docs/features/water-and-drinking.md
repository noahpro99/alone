Staying hydrated is a constant chore: your thirst only ever drops, and the water you find can make you sick unless you treat it.

## The thirst meter

The blue bar in your HUD is thirst. Unlike hunger, it has no natural way to climb on its own — it **only drains**, and you top it back up by drinking.

- It falls steadily over time.
- It falls **faster in the heat** and when you exert yourself (sprinting, hard work). A hot body sweats, and sweat costs water.
- Let it run low and you feel it: **Weakness** as it gets low, then **Slowness** and fatigue when it hits empty.

Because thirst never refills for free, water is something you have to plan around — especially before a long trip or a stint in the desert.

## Drinking

Point at a water source and **right-click bare-handed** to drink straight from it. It refills thirst, but drinking untreated water is a gamble:

- Raw water from a river, lake, or pond carries a **~15% chance to make you sick** each time you drink.
- Getting sick means a lingering foodborne illness — not something you want to roll the dice on repeatedly.

The safe way to drink is to treat your water first (boiling), or collect clean rainwater. See the water-quality table below.

## Water quality

Every drink and every vessel has a quality. It decides how likely a sip is to make you ill.

| Quality | Where it comes from | Sickness chance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Raw / murky** | Straight from a river, lake, or pond | ~15% |
| **Tainted / dirty** | Filled into a dirty vessel, or drunk after handling raw meat | ~45% |
| **Clean** | Boiled, or caught as rainwater | Safe (0%) |
| **Salt** | Ocean, beach, or shore water | Dehydrates you — never drink it untreated |

## Vessels

Carrying water lets you drink on the move instead of hunting for a source every time you're thirsty. There are two vessels:

- **Waterskin** — crafted from leather and string. Holds **3 sips**.
- **Iron pot** — crafted from iron. Holds **6 sips** — a real upgrade for travel and desert crossings.

Both work the same way:

- **Right-click a water source** to fill the vessel.
- **Right-click while holding it** to drink. Drinking from a vessel is a **timed action (~1.6 seconds)**, not instant — you have to stand and drink.
- Each vessel shows a **water gauge** (like a durability bar) tinted by quality, so you can read at a glance how much water is left and what kind it is: **blue** for clean, **murky** for raw, **sickly** for tainted, **teal** for salt.

## Boiling water clean

Boiling is *the* way to make untreated water safe.

- Set a **fire-safe vessel** (the iron pot) over a **lit campfire**.
- After a few seconds it comes to a boil and turns its whole load **clean** — raw or tainted water becomes safe to drink.
- Boiling also **sterilises the vessel itself**, so a dirty vessel comes out clean.

Boiling **salt water desalinates it** to clean, and as a bonus yields a **salt** item (used to preserve food — see [Food & Eating](./food-and-eating.md)).

## Keep two vessels

Vessels remember whether they're dirty, and dirt spreads:

- **Sipping raw or tainted water dirties the vessel.**
- A **dirty vessel taints the next fill** — even clean-looking water goes tainted (~45% sickness).
- **Boiling sterilises the vessel**, clearing the dirt.

The practical habit — the same one real survivalists use — is to carry **two vessels**: one dirty vessel for *collecting* water from the wild, and one clean vessel you only ever drink treated water from. Never confuse them.

## Salt water

Ocean, beach, and shore water is **salt water**. Drinking it — bare-handed *or* from a vessel — **dehydrates you** instead of quenching your thirst. Rivers and lakes stay fresh; only the sea is salt.

To make seawater drinkable, fill a vessel and **boil it** to desalinate it (and pocket the leftover salt). At the coast with no fresh water, that boil is your lifeline.

## Rain catch — free clean water

A **water cauldron** fills from rain (as it does in vanilla), and it's a **fuel-free source of clean water**:

- **Right-click it bare-handed** to drink clean straight from it — no sickness, spends a level.
- **Right-click it with a vessel** to fill the vessel **clean** and drain the cauldron.

Setting out a cauldron under the open sky is the cheapest reliable clean water you can get — no fire, no fuel, no risk.
